A year into my time at CareMessage, I proposed we build a proper design system. Token-based, component-library-backed, the whole thing. I made a Figma deck. I had a rollout plan. I shared it in a design review and got polite nods.

Then nothing happened.

Not because the idea was bad. But because I misread the room.

## The team wasn't in pain yet

A design system solves problems of scale and consistency. But when I proposed it, we had one designer (me), two product squads, and a codebase that moved fast enough that locking down components felt like overhead, not help.

The engineers weren't frustrated by inconsistency. The PMs weren't asking for it. And without a shared pain point, a design system is just a designer's pet project.

## What I should have done

The move wasn't to propose a system. It was to solve one specific problem visibly — a component that kept getting reimplemented slightly differently across screens, a color that appeared in three variations, a spacing inconsistency that confused engineers during handoff.

Fix that one thing, name it, document it lightly. Let the team feel the value before you ask for the investment.

## A year later

The team grew. We added more designers and more engineers. The inconsistencies compounded. And the design system conversation came back up — not from me, but from a frustrated engineer who had implemented the same modal three different ways.

That's when we finally built it. And it landed because everyone already felt the problem.

Timing matters as much as the idea. Sometimes your job is to plant the seed and wait for the soil to be ready.
